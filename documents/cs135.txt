Hi I’m not sure what your major is but like most people have said, 135 tends to be a weed out class. For some people, it’s pretty easy but I’d say a majority of the class tends to struggle with it. It’s very common for people to take this class twice and there are a lot of resources to help you but you need to find them and be aware of them. The information, at first, is very overwhelming especially if you’ve never coded before. If you’re trying to fulfill your CO12 requirement, take SCI 425 (or something like that). It’s a 1 credit class that I’ve heard is pretty easy.

I personally would never recommend this class to anyone that wanted an easier alternative or an easy A. If you are an engineering (specifically CS, EE, BME), math, or physics major then I can see why your advisor recommended this course for you. Otherwise, you’re taking this for fun. Coding is a very valuable skill but be ready to put the work in for this class.

I’m not a STEM major but I decided to take CS135 just to gain some coding knowledge. Imo I’ve learned a lot from that class and I don’t regret taking it. Like others have stated, the class is pretty straightforward.

I had Davis instead of Keith but the contents, projects, and labs are the same. Davis is extremely helpful and will help you understand the concepts at a more simplistic level - she’s awesome!

I will say: the lectures don’t necessarily help you with the Programming Assignments, but there are many sources (Prof, TAs, TFs) that you can seek help from. It’s a pretty easy class; with all the Programming Assignments, exams, and labs, I had a high A. The only thing that screwed my grade was the final group project. The worst part is that you don’t get to choose your group members so just hope that you’ll have hardworking peers.

I wouldn't judge the quality of any program by the difficulty of my experience alone, I am 36 years old and have developed good time and stress management that has helped tremendously.

The material was adequately covered in lecture, and it was easy to apply it to the assignments. All in all it took about 30 hrs of my time outside of class, and the lectures were about 35 hours. I'm sure others have had a different experience, a lot of people in the class failed the tests, which had 60% averages before the curve. I just didn't find it at all difficult, and looked forward to the assignments like a fun puzzle.
